# **📘 The Mesh — Classroom Syllabus**
### *Crisis Simulation, Systems Thinking, and Network Leadership*

---

## **Course Overview**
**The Mesh** is an experiential crisis‑simulation course where students learn how complex systems behave under stress — and how collaborative teams stabilize them. Through gameplay, reflection, and analysis, students develop skills in:

- systems thinking  
- cross‑disciplinary collaboration  
- crisis leadership  
- trust and reputation dynamics  
- resource management  
- negotiation and governance  
- resilience under uncertainty  

The course blends simulation turns, team strategy sessions, lectures, and reflective analysis.

---

## **Learning Objectives**
By the end of the course, students will be able to:

1. Analyze how crises propagate through interconnected systems.  
2. Evaluate trust, resources, and influence as dynamic network variables.  
3. Collaborate across disciplines to stabilize complex environments.  
4. Apply negotiation, coalition‑building, and governance strategies.  
5. Reflect on leadership behaviors under pressure.  
6. Map network changes and identify emergent patterns.  
7. Communicate decisions clearly and justify strategic choices.  

---

## **Course Format**
The course runs in **weekly modules**, each combining:

- **Simulation Turns** (live gameplay)  
- **Team Strategy Sessions**  
- **Mini‑Lectures** (systems thinking, crisis leadership, etc.)  
- **Reflection & Debrief**  
- **Assignments** (analysis, mapping, strategy write‑ups)  

---

# **Weekly Breakdown**

---

## **Week 1 — Introduction to The Mesh**
- Course overview  
- Systems thinking fundamentals  
- Network basics: nodes, edges, trust, resources, influence  
- Onboarding wizard + quickstart guide  
- First demo turn  

**Assignment:** Reflection: “What surprised you about the network?”

---

## **Week 2 — Trust & Influence Dynamics**
- How trust shapes decision-making  
- Influence pathways and cascading effects  
- Action categories overview  
- Simulation Turn 1  

**Assignment:** Trust map of your node and adjacent teams.

---

## **Week 3 — Resource Management Under Stress**
- Scarcity, pooling, bidding  
- Operational capacity and crisis fatigue  
- Simulation Turn 2  

**Assignment:** Resource strategy memo.

---

## **Week 4 — Coalition Formation & Negotiation**
- Coalition-building theory  
- Mediation vs. arbitration  
- Cross-team negotiation workshop  
- Simulation Turn 3  

**Assignment:** Coalition proposal (team-based).

---

## **Week 5 — Governance & Decision Architecture**
- Emergency protocols  
- Referendums  
- Distributed vs. centralized decision-making  
- Simulation Turn 4  

**Assignment:** Draft an emergency protocol.

---

## **Week 6 — Intelligence & Information Actions**
- Network scans  
- Intelligence sprints  
- Forecasting uncertainty  
- Simulation Turn 5  

**Assignment:** Crisis forecast (individual).

---

## **Week 7 — Technical & Infrastructure Crises**
- Patch deployment  
- Protocol rewrite  
- Cyber and IT failure modes  
- Simulation Turn 6  

**Assignment:** Technical incident response plan.

---

## **Week 8 — ESG, Ethics & Sustainability**
- Ethical sourcing  
- Carbon rebalance  
- Social trust and reputational risk  
- Simulation Turn 7  

**Assignment:** Ethical audit report.

---

## **Week 9 — Meta‑Game Actions & System Evolution**
- Node merging  
- Knowledge pooling  
- Long-term system stability  
- Simulation Turn 8  

**Assignment:** Network evolution analysis.

---

## **Week 10 — Final Simulation & Capstone**
- Extended multi-turn crisis  
- Teams present strategies  
- Full-class debrief  
- Leadership reflection  

**Final Project:**  
**“How Our System Survived (or Collapsed)”** — a 6–10 page analysis of network behavior, team strategy, and crisis outcomes.

---

# **Assessment & Grading**

### **Participation (30%)**
Engagement in simulation turns, discussions, and team strategy.

### **Weekly Assignments (30%)**
Short reflections, maps, memos, and analyses.

### **Coalition/Team Deliverables (10%)**
Group strategy artifacts.

### **Final Project (30%)**
Comprehensive analysis of crisis behavior and leadership.

---

# **Instructor Guidance**
- Keep pace brisk — momentum matters.  
- Encourage cross-team communication.  
- Let failure happen — collapse is a powerful learning moment.  
- Use debrief questions to deepen insight.  
- Connect gameplay to real-world systems:  
  - supply chains  
  - campus governance  
  - organizational behavior  
  - crisis leadership  
  - cybersecurity  
  - sustainability
