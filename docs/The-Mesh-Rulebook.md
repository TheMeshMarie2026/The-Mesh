📘 The Mesh Rulebook
A Crisis‑Simulation Game for Corporate & University Teams
The Mesh is a heterarchical crisis‑simulation game where teams represent nodes in a living network. Every decision you make changes the network’s trust, resources, and influence pathways — shaping how the crisis unfolds.

Your goal is simple:
Collaborate across disciplines to stabilize the system before the crisis overwhelms it.

This rulebook explains every action type you may encounter during play.

1. Coalition & Coordination Actions
These actions represent teams forming alliances, working groups, or cross‑functional coalitions.

Coalition Formation
Teams formally join forces to tackle part of the crisis together. Coalitions increase trust and strengthen influence pathways between members.

Knowledge Exchange
Teams share expertise across disciplines (engineering ↔ finance, students ↔ faculty, etc.). This boosts trust and improves network resilience.

2. Resource Dynamics Actions
These actions simulate real-world logistics, scarcity, and resource management.

Resource Pooling
Multiple teams contribute resources to a shared emergency pool. This increases collective capacity but costs each contributor slightly.

Resource Bidding
Teams compete for scarce resources. Higher-trust teams often gain an advantage, reflecting real organizational dynamics.

3. Trust & Reputation Actions
Trust is the real currency of The Mesh. These actions shape how teams perceive each other.

Trust Pledge
Teams make commitments to future actions. Keeping pledges builds trust; breaking them can cause cascading penalties.

Reputation Broadcast
A team publicly signals its reliability or achievements, influencing how others trust and follow its lead.

4. Governance & Policy Actions
These actions change how the network makes decisions.

Emergency Protocol
Teams draft temporary governance rules to stabilize the crisis. This often boosts trust in the authors.

Referendum
All teams vote on a major decision. Voting increases trust and can shift the crisis direction.

5. Information & Intelligence Actions
Crises thrive on uncertainty. These actions reduce it.

Network Scan
A team analyzes the network to reveal vulnerabilities or hidden dependencies.

Intelligence Sprint
Teams rapidly gather external data (market signals, campus sentiment, technical diagnostics). This boosts trust and improves decision quality.

6. Conflict & Negotiation Actions
Not all crises are external — some are interpersonal or organizational.

Mediation
A neutral team helps resolve conflict between two others, improving trust and stabilizing edges between them.

Arbitration
A designated authority resolves a dispute. Both parties gain trust, and the arbitrator gains influence.

7. ESG & Ethical Actions
These actions reflect environmental, social, and governance responsibilities.

Carbon Rebalance
Teams redistribute carbon impact or sustainability load. This can improve resource capacity and public trust.

Ethical Sourcing Audit
Teams investigate supply chain ethics. Positive findings increase trust; negative findings may shift crisis severity.

8. Technical Infrastructure Actions
These actions simulate cyber, IT, and protocol crises.

Patch Deployment
Teams deploy a fix to a technical vulnerability. This increases trust in technical teams and reduces crisis severity.

Protocol Rewrite
Teams update or rewrite a governance or technical protocol. This increases influence and can reshape network behavior.

9. Meta‑Game Actions
These actions affect the future of the crisis, not just the present.

Crisis Forecast
Teams predict future crisis developments. Accurate forecasting increases trust and resilience.

Node Merge
Two teams combine into a single, more powerful node. Their resources and trust merge, creating a new strategic actor.

10. Classroom & Team Actions
These actions are designed for educational or workshop settings.

Team Negotiation
Teams negotiate directly with each other. Successful negotiation increases trust and stabilizes edges.

Knowledge Pooling
Teams combine research, insights, or analysis. This boosts trust and improves network-wide decision-making.

How Actions Affect the Network
Every action changes three core attributes:

Resource Capacity
Represents time, money, energy, or operational bandwidth.

Trust
Represents reliability, credibility, and social capital.

Influence Weight
Represents how strongly one team’s decisions affect another.

Small changes in these values can cascade across the network — strengthening or destabilizing the system.

How to Play
Receive the crisis narrative.  
The narrator describes what’s happening.

Review your three choices.  
Each choice is a real organizational action.

Discuss with your team.  
Consider trust, resources, and influence pathways.

Choose one action.  
Your decision updates the network graph.

Watch the crisis evolve.  
New narratives emerge based on your actions.

Repeat until stability or collapse.  
The Mesh rewards collaboration, not hierarchy.

Winning The Mesh
You “win” by stabilizing the network:

Trust is high

Resources are balanced

Influence pathways are strong

Crisis severity stops escalating

You “lose” when:

Trust collapses

Resources deplete

Influence pathways break

Crisis severity spirals out of control

The Mesh is not about beating other teams — it’s about learning how complex systems survive through collaboration.
